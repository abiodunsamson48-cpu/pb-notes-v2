Many people pray for a godly relationship, but few truly prepare for one. We often focus on who we want to marry rather than who we are becoming. Yet the quality of your future relationship is shaped by the quality of your life with God today.

A godly relationship is not stumbled upon. It is built intentionally, guided by Scripture, and sustained through wisdom. In this first part, we will focus on the foundation every relationship must rest upon.

### Your Relationship with God Comes First
Before you think about a partner, you must settle your walk with God. Your relationship with God is not just important, it is the foundation of every other relationship in your life.

If God is not central to you, it will be difficult to live rightly, love sacrificially, forgive genuinely, and remain faithful when emotions are tested. God is not just someone you invite into your relationship; He is the third person in it.

> "Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters." — Colossians 3:23

In your relationship with God, you learn what real love looks like: sacrificial, patient, and committed (**John 3:16; John 15:12**). You also learn what true forgiveness is: not keeping records of wrongs but extending grace (**Hebrews 10:17; Matthew 18:22**). 

If you have not truly experienced God's love, you will struggle to give that same love to another person. You cannot give what you do not have.

**What to do next:** Before pursuing anyone romantically, strengthen your personal devotion life. Commit to consistent prayer, studying Scripture, and accountability with a mature believer. Let God shape your heart before you invite someone else into it.

### Choosing a Partner God's Way
The world says, "Choose who excites you." God says, "Choose who aligns with My purpose for you." Attraction is not wrong, but it is not enough. Character matters more.

When considering a partner, ask:
* Are they genuinely born again? (**2 Corinthians 6:14**)
* Do they display godly character and integrity? (**Proverbs 31:10**)
* Are they growing spiritually or just going through religious routines?
* Do they share your values and vision for life?

### Understanding God's Plan in Relationships
Your spouse is like an anointing packaged into a person, someone fitted for you by God. However, marriage requires both people to cooperate with God's will. 

If you are ready but the other person says no, that does not mean you missed God. It simply means God is not finished yet. You cannot coerce anyone to marry you, you cannot force anyone to love you, and you must not remain stuck where God is no longer leading. When one door closes, trust that God can open another.

**What to do next:** Write down your non-negotiables based on Scripture, not feelings, and pray them back to God. Let His Word, not your emotions, guide your choices.

Building a godly relationship begins with building yourself in God. When your foundation is right, your choices become clearer, your boundaries stronger, and your discernment sharper.
